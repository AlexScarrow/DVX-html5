# Derples vs Xenos Monetization Plan (Steam-First)

## 1) Executive Recommendation

Use a **Steam-first premium model** for initial commercialization:

- Primary model: **buy-to-play** on Steam/Steam Deck.
- Secondary model: optional **free web demo** as top-of-funnel marketing.
- Avoid pay-per-play tokens for v1 (high friction and high ops complexity).

This route offers the best balance of:

- lower legal/compliance burden,
- lower backend/payment overhead,
- better player trust and conversion,
- clearer product positioning.

## 2) Product Positioning

Target positioning:

- Turn-based tactical co-op with strong aesthetic identity.
- Premium indie game with replayability and social multiplayer value.

Core value messaging:

- "One purchase, full game access."
- "Co-op planning and tactical decision-making."
- "Frequent content/polish updates post-launch."

## 3) Business Model Options (Recommended + Deferred)

### Recommended for v1

1. **Premium Base Game (Steam)**
   - Single upfront purchase.
   - Includes multiplayer features.

2. **Optional Web Demo (No Tokens)**
   - Limited content (e.g., tutorial + 1 map + capped progression).
   - CTA to wishlist/purchase on Steam.

### Defer until product-market fit

- Paid DLC campaigns/classes.
- Cosmetic packs.
- Season passes/live-service loops.

### Avoid in v1

- Pay-per-play tokens.
- Real-money prize mechanics.
- Any cash-economy features requiring KYC/AML/fraud handling.

## 4) Suggested Pricing Strategy

Use staged pricing:

- **Launch price band:** low-mid indie premium tier.
- **Launch discount:** small opening-week discount to boost conversion and review velocity.
- **Sale cadence:** participate in major Steam seasonal sales once baseline reviews stabilize.

Pricing should be validated by:

- content depth at launch,
- session length/replayability,
- multiplayer reliability and onboarding quality.

## 5) Revenue Levers (Priority Order)

1. **Core conversion quality**
   - trailer/store page clarity,
   - polished onboarding,
   - strong first-session retention.

2. **Review quality and volume**
   - bug stability and UX polish drive positive reviews.

3. **Wishlist conversion at launch**
   - wishlists are the strongest launch multiplier.

4. **Post-launch update cadence**
   - meaningful updates drive long-tail sales.

5. **Later content monetization**
   - DLC/cosmetics only after proving retention.

## 6) Lightweight Financial Framework

Track the following as leading indicators:

- Wishlist growth rate (weekly)
- Demo-to-wishlist conversion (if web demo is used)
- Store page conversion rate
- Refund rate
- Review score + review count
- 7-day and 30-day retention
- Multiplayer session completion rate

Use three forecast cases:

- **Conservative:** low conversion, high refund risk.
- **Expected:** moderate conversion, stable review trend.
- **Upside:** strong review velocity + streamer/social pickup.

## 7) Go-To-Market Funnel (Practical)

1. Build public awareness:
   - short clips, devlogs, Steam page updates.
2. Drive wishlists pre-launch:
   - demo footage and tactical gameplay hooks.
3. Launch with stability:
   - no major multiplayer blockers.
4. Post-launch updates:
   - bugfix sprint, then content/quality beats.

## 8) Risk Register and Mitigation

### Risk: Low conversion at launch

Mitigation:

- Improve store page clarity and trailer pacing.
- Focus messaging on tactical co-op differentiators.

### Risk: Multiplayer friction harms reviews

Mitigation:

- QA host/join reliability before release.
- Clear reconnect/error UX.

### Risk: Scope creep before monetization proof

Mitigation:

- Keep v1 features focused.
- Delay complex economic systems.

## 9) Decision Summary

For this game and team stage:

- **Best near-term commercial route:** Steam premium buy-to-play.
- **Best optional adjunct:** free web demo for discovery.
- **Not recommended for v1:** tokenized per-match monetization.
